🔴 Immediate — Do This Month

💰

Open a High-Yield Business Savings Account

$91,049 is sitting in a non-interest-bearing TD checking account. A business savings account at 4%+ APY would generate $2,600–$3,600/year in free interest — with zero effort. Keep $25K in checking for operations, sweep the rest. At projected year-end cash of $52K+, this is money left on the table every single day.

IMPACT: +$2,600–$3,600/YEAR
📋

Document All Venmo & Check Payments Immediately

Over 26 months, $27,000+ went out via Venmo and $25,000+ via checks — with no payee information visible on bank statements. As a 501(c)(3), the IRS requires documentation of payments exceeding $600 to any individual (1099 reporting). This is a compliance risk that should be remediated before the next tax filing. Create a simple log: Date | Recipient | Amount | Purpose.

IMPACT: COMPLIANCE RISK MITIGATION
🔍

Identify Check #1190 — $15,086 (June 2024)

This is the single largest disbursement in 26 months of data. No vendor name is visible on the bank statement. The board should confirm what this payment was for, whether it recurs, and ensure proper documentation exists. At $15K, this represents 20%+ of annual expenses.

🟡 This Season — Before April Orders

👕

Get 3 Competitive Bids on Uniforms Before Ordering

Athletes Alley (Shrewsbury) has received $37,316 over 2 years — averaging $18,658/year. This is your single largest expense category. Before placing the 2026 order, solicit quotes from BSN Sports, Squad Locker, and a local screen printer. Even a 12% reduction saves $2,200+/year.

IMPACT: SAVE $1,500–$2,500/YEAR
📊

Cancel or Downgrade SurveyMonkey ($99/mo → $0-$25/mo)

You've paid $1,683 for SurveyMonkey since May 2025. The organization ran the entire 2024 season without it. Google Forms provides identical survey functionality for free. If surveys are essential, SurveyMonkey Basic at $25/mo covers most needs.

IMPACT: SAVE $888–$1,188/YEAR
⚾

Understand the 27% League Dues Increase

Two Rivers Little League dues jumped from $10,292 (2024) to $13,068 (2025) — a $2,776 increase. Is this driven by player count, per-player fee increases, or new assessments? Understanding the driver helps you forecast accurately and potentially negotiate.

🟢 Strategic — Build Over 12 Months

🏢

Pursue Corporate Matching Grants — $5K–$10K Opportunity

CyberGrants deposited $4,100 in 2024 (Jul + Oct) — corporate matching gifts from employers. This is untapped potential. Many NJ employers (J&J, Merck, Prudential, Goldman) match charitable donations to youth sports 1:1. Register on Benevity and Double the Donation. Send one email to all parents: "Does your employer match?"

IMPACT: +$5,000–$10,000/YEAR
🎉

Revive Fundraising Events

2024 saw $18,197 flow through Venmo in June (likely event/sponsorship proceeds). This didn't repeat in 2025. A single annual event — golf outing, trivia night, home run derby — can generate $3,000–$8,000 net. The Fair Haven/Rumson community has the disposable income to support it.

IMPACT: +$3,000–$8,000/YEAR
🏪

Launch Local Business Sponsorship Program

You're in one of NJ's most affluent communities. Local businesses want community visibility. Offer: jersey sponsors ($300–$500/team), field banner ads ($200–$500/season), scoreboard naming ($1,000+). With 12-15 teams, even modest participation generates meaningful non-registration revenue.

IMPACT: +$3,000–$8,000/YEAR
📜

Establish a Formal Reserve Policy

The board should adopt a resolution: "FHCB will maintain a minimum unrestricted operating reserve of $30,000 (~7 months of expenses)." Current cash of $91K far exceeds this. The surplus above reserve ($61K+) can be intentionally deployed for program improvement — field upgrades, equipment, scholarship fund, coaching development.
